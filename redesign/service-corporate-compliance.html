What We Do

Corporate Compliance

"Miss an ASIC deadline and you'll face late fees — and in serious cases, deregistration. We watch the dates so you don't have to."

If your business is registered as a company, you have ongoing legal obligations with ASIC — Australia's corporate regulator. Many small business owners don't realise these obligations exist until they receive a penalty notice. We act as your registered office and handle everything on your behalf, so you stay compliant without the stress. Think of us as your company's admin backbone, working in the background to keep everything in order.

What's Included

ASIC Compliance Management

We monitor all your ASIC obligations and deadlines, ensuring every requirement is met on time — no nasty surprises, no late fees.

Registered Office Services

We act as your official registered office address — the address required by law for ASIC correspondence and public company documents.

Corporate Register Maintenance

We keep your company's register of members, directors, and share interests accurate and up to date — as required under the Corporations Act.

Officeholder Updates & Changes

Director appointments, resignations, address changes — we lodge all ASIC notifications within the required 28-day window, every time.

Annual Statement Reviews

We review your ASIC annual statement each year, confirm all details are correct, and arrange payment of the annual review fee on your behalf.

ASIC Fee Payments

We manage the payment of ASIC annual review fees so they're never missed — avoiding the late payment penalties that can quickly add up.

ASIC Liaison on Your Behalf

Any correspondence from ASIC comes to us first. We interpret it, respond where needed, and only contact you when there's something that genuinely needs your attention.

Company Deregistration Services

If you're winding down a company, we handle the deregistration process with ASIC correctly — avoiding ongoing fees and compliance obligations.

Who It Suits

Ideal for companies of all sizes — from newly incorporated entities that need to establish proper compliance processes, to existing companies looking to hand over the administrative burden to professionals and ensure nothing is missed. If you've recently set up a company, or if you're not sure whether your ASIC obligations are up to date, this service gives you peace of mind without the complexity.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

You're not alone — many small business owners set up a company without fully understanding the ongoing obligations. Every registered Australian company must maintain a registered office address, keep a corporate register, notify ASIC of any changes to directors or addresses within 28 days, and pay an annual review fee. The good news is that all of this can be handed to us, so you don't have to worry about any of it.
Every Australian company must have a registered office — this is the official address where ASIC correspondence is sent and where the public can inspect company documents during business hours. CYS Accountants can serve as your registered office, ensuring all official correspondence is handled promptly and professionally.
Missing ASIC deadlines can result in late fees and penalties. In serious cases, ASIC can deregister your company — which creates significant problems if you're still trading. Our team tracks all your compliance deadlines and will notify you well in advance so you're never caught out. We make corporate compliance stress-free.
While private companies in Australia are no longer required to have a company secretary, maintaining proper corporate governance remains important. We can advise on best practices and ensure your company meets all its obligations under the Corporations Act without the overhead of a full-time appointment.
Changes to company details — such as officeholder appointments, resignations, or address changes — must be notified to ASIC within specified timeframes (often 28 days). Our team handles all these notifications on your behalf, ensuring they're lodged correctly and on time every time.
Deregistering a company with ASIC involves confirming there are no outstanding debts, that the company has ceased trading, and lodging the appropriate form. Done correctly, it stops the annual review fees and removes ongoing obligations. Done incorrectly or left undone, you'll keep receiving ASIC bills and may face personal liability issues. We handle the whole process to make sure it's finalised properly.
